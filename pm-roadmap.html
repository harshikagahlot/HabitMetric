Phase 0 — 5-Day Starter Sprint

Do this before anything else. If you skip this, the rest of the roadmap won't stick.

Day 1 — Honest Self-Assessment + Setup + Resume Draft

Answer in writing (not in your head): Why PM over SDE? What problem does HabitMetric solve? Name 3 competitor apps.

  • Create a Notion workspace called "PM Lab"
  • Set up a free Figma account
  • Create a Google Sheet to track learning + deliverables
  • Read 3 blog posts from practicing PMs (not courses)
🎯 Internship Task — Day 1
  • Draft your resume today — see Section 12 (Resume Blueprint) for the exact template
  • Set up profiles on: LinkedIn, Internshala, Unstop, Wellfound (AngelList), Weekday.works
  • Create a tracker spreadsheet: Company | Role | Applied Date | Status | Contact Person | Follow-up Date

Day 2 — Real User Research (Your First PM Skill)

Talk to 5 real people — 2 who tried a habit tracker and quit, 2 who never used one, 1 person 25+ years old.

  • "Tell me about the last time you tried to build a habit. What happened?"
  • "Have you used an app for this? What made you stop?"
  • "What would make you open a habit app every single day?"
  • Write verbatim quotes in Notion — don't interpret yet
🎯 Internship Task — Day 2
  • Search these NOW on Internshala: "Product Intern", "Growth Intern", "Business Analyst Intern", "Founder's Office" — save 10+ listings to your tracker
  • Search Wellfound for early-stage startups (Seed–Series B) in EdTech, Health, Consumer apps — these are your best shots

Day 3 — Synthesize Research + Write First User Persona

Using yesterday's interviews, write 2 User Personas in Notion in full sentences — not bullet templates.

  • Persona 1: "The Ambitious Quitter" — motivated starter, chronic stopper
  • Persona 2: "The First Timer" — never seriously tried habit tracking
  • Each persona: situation, pain point (their exact words), what they want, past attempts, biggest fear
🎯 Internship Task — Day 3
  • Find 5 founders/PMs at startups on LinkedIn. Send a thoughtful connection request — not "please give me internship", but something specific: "I'm building HabitMetric and studied how Jar handles streak motivation — would love to learn how you think about retention."
  • Target: founders at companies with 10–100 employees in consumer apps or edtech

Day 4 — Product Teardown (Habitica + One Indian App)

Use each app actively for 30 minutes. Then write a structured analysis in Notion.

  • What user problem does each primarily solve?
  • What does each do better than HabitMetric?
  • What feature would you cut if you were the PM?
  • Choose one Indian app: CRED, Groww, Jar, or Zepto
🎯 Internship Task — Day 4
  • Apply to 2–3 internships TODAY — don't wait for the perfect resume. Applying early = better. Use your draft resume + HabitMetric GitHub link.
  • Target for today: Internshala listings for "Product Analyst" or "Growth" at companies with <200 employees. These are approachable and reply fast.

Day 5 — Write the HabitMetric Problem Statement + Cover Note Template

400 words in Notion. Structure: The specific problem → Who has it → How people cope today → Your solution hypothesis → 2–3 metrics that prove it's working.

  • Example North Star hypothesis: "If users understand WHY they fail — not just WHETHER they failed — they stay consistent longer"
  • Define what "working" means in measurable terms before writing code is the PM mindset shift
🎯 Internship Task — Day 5
  • Write your master cover note (100–150 words): "I'm a 3rd year BTech student building HabitMetric — a habit tracker that uses drop-risk intelligence to reduce streak abandonment. I have [X] user interviews done, a published PRD, and a deployed product. I'm deeply interested in how [Company] approaches [specific area]. Would love to contribute to [specific team/product problem]."
  • Customize this template for every application — never send the generic version

How to Apply — The Right Method

📩 The Outreach Formula That Works

  • Find the hiring manager or founder on LinkedIn
  • Like/comment on 1–2 of their posts first (genuine, not sycophantic)
  • Then send a connection request with a personalized note
  • After connecting, send a 3-sentence DM: (1) Who you are + what you build, (2) Why specifically their company, (3) One specific ask
  • Attach your resume + Notion case study link in every DM

🎯 Cold Email Template (for companies with no open posting)

  • Subject: "BTech student building HabitMetric — interested in contributing to [Company] product team"
  • Para 1: What you're building + one specific insight about their product
  • Para 2: One specific way you can help (not generic)
  • Para 3: "I've attached my resume and a link to my case study. Happy to jump on a 15-min call if it makes sense."
  • Keep it under 150 words. Long emails don't get read.

🏆 Competitions = Fast Resume Builders

  • Unstop product case competitions — join 1–2 per month
  • IIM Ahmedabad case competitions (open to BTech students)
  • Meesho, CRED, Zepto brand challenges — companies recruit directly from winners
  • Even top-3 placement in a small competition is worth adding to resume
  • These force you to do structured PM thinking — great practice

⚡ Volume vs Quality — The Right Balance

  • Month 1: 3–5 applications/week (volume, any relevant role)
  • Month 2–3: 5–8/week (volume + targeted outreach)
  • Month 4+: 8–10/week + active referral asks
  • Rule: Never send a generic cover note. Customize every single one.
  • Track everything in your spreadsheet — patterns will emerge

Mistakes Beginners Make — Avoid All of These

The patterns that waste months of effort

Mistake 1: Collecting Certificates Instead of Building Skills

PM certificates from Coursera or LinkedIn Learning mean nothing in isolation. What matters is whether you can do a teardown, write a PRD, and defend your decisions. The only way to learn that is by practicing — not watching videos.

Mistake 2: Treating Case Studies as Templates to Fill

Interviewers have seen 500 templated case studies. They want your specific reasoning — not a filled template. Your HabitMetric decisions (localStorage vs backend, Habit Score vs streak counter) are more valuable than a formatted "Zomato redesign" deck.

Mistake 3: Cold Applying Without a Portfolio

Sending a resume to Swiggy PM intern with no deliverables is noise. They receive 3,000+ applications. Without a referral or portfolio link, you are competing purely on college name.

Mistake 4: Can't Explain Technical Work in PM Language

You built localStorage sync. A PM answer is: "I evaluated localStorage vs a backend database and chose localStorage initially because cross-device sync wasn't a Day-1 user need, and the engineering cost of a backend would have delayed core feature validation by 3 weeks." That sentence gets you hired. "I used localStorage" doesn't.

Mistake 5: LinkedIn That Looks Like an SDE Profile with "Interested in PM"

This communicates confusion. Your LinkedIn must tell one coherent story: builder who thinks in products. Remove the contradiction.

The 3-Second Self-Test for Every Feature
